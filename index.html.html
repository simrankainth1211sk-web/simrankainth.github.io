DePaul University · ECO 520 · 2024
Socioeconomic Drivers of Smoking in Chicago
Solo research SAS · R

Analyzed adult smoking rates across all 77 Chicago community areas using the Chicago Health Atlas dataset (2018–2023). Built a full analytics pipeline from descriptive statistics through predictive modeling to understand how poverty, education, income, and health access shape smoking behavior at the neighborhood level.

Key findings
  • Poverty rate was the strongest predictor — accounting for 41% of smoking rate variability (ANOVA F = 14.94, p < 0.0001)
  • K-means clustering (8 clusters) revealed high-poverty, low-education areas had up to 2× the smoking rates of affluent neighborhoods
  • Optimized Random Forest model achieved the best predictive accuracy (R² = 26.3%, MSE = 20.95), outperforming all regression baselines
  • Neural Network Model 2 (AUC = 0.752, specificity = 88.9%) effectively identified high-risk community areas

DePaul University · ECO 535 · 2024
Behavioral Interventions for School Absenteeism in India
Co-authored with Jacob Kauppila
Policy research Behavioral econ

Designed a suite of evidence-based behavioral interventions to address chronic school absenteeism in India — a problem rooted in poverty, child labor, gender norms, and weak school infrastructure. Applied behavioral economics frameworks (prospect theory, hyperbolic discounting, social norms) to explain why families keep children out of school, then translated empirical evidence from Kenya, the US, and India into actionable policy proposals.

Interventions designed
  • Positive + negative reminder campaigns via SMS and letters (leverages loss aversion and timely nudges)
  • Conditional income transfers linked to attendance (addresses economic barriers; tested against Kenya RCT evidence)
  • Automatic school enrollment as the default option (default-effects from behavioral economics)
  • Teacher training in positive reinforcement, bias awareness, and attendance incentives
  • Public recognition programs to build social norms around regular attendance
